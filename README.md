# AI Infrastructure Research Framework

A structured research framework for evaluating AI infrastructure investment themes through evidence validation, scenario analysis, and decision mapping.

This project is not intended to provide stock recommendations. Instead, it is designed to show how AI infrastructure bottlenecks can be researched, classified, and converted into repeatable investment research decisions.

## Research Objective

The core question of this project is:

> How should long-term investors evaluate structural bottlenecks in AI infrastructure?

The framework focuses on power availability, grid constraints, cooling, networking, data centers, utilities, and other infrastructure layers that may affect AI deployment over the next decade.

## Methodology

Each module follows a repeatable research process:

1. Define the core thesis question.
2. Collect evidence from primary and authoritative sources.
3. Classify evidence by type, reliability, geography, and time horizon.
4. Identify what the evidence supports and what remains uncertain.
5. Convert the research into a decision framework.
